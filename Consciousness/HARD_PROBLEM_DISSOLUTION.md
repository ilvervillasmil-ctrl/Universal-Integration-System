### Part 1: The Hard Problem of Consciousness (Chalmers, 1995)

**The Question:**
"Why is the performance of cognitive functions accompanied by subjective experience?"

**Examples:**
- Why does seeing red FEEL like something?
- Why aren't we philosophical zombies (functioning identically but with no inner experience)?
- What is the explanatory gap between neural processes and phenomenology?

**Traditional Approaches:**
- Reductionism: Experience = neural activity (fails: doesn't explain WHY)
- Functionalism: Experience = computational states (fails: Chinese Room objection)
- Emergentism: Experience emerges from complexity (fails: vague, no mechanism)
- Panpsychism: Everything has experience (fails: combination problem)
- Eliminativism: Experience doesn't exist (fails: self-refuting)

**Why It's "Hard":**
- Explaining function doesn't explain feeling
- Third-person descriptions don't capture first-person perspective
- Seems irreducible to physical processes
